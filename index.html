Exploring Terrorism

This page visualizes and describes the story of terrorism. It highlights a number of key factors and examines relationships in depth. Data gathered from a government body located in USA.

Terrorist Attacks over Time

From the time series area chart below we can see that over time the trend of terrorist attacks is upwards. I would say that there seems to be almost a cyclical pattern forming but would need to wait for future data to confirm this structure.

I've annotated the point 2001 as that marks the infamous 9/11 attack that devastated the world globally. It's interesting to note that the few years after 2001, attacks spiked upwards, perhaps as a result of this event. I've also annotated 2014 which marks the highest activity of terrorist attacks, and the infamous Lindt Cafe Siege in Sydney. I remember that holiday season I was living in Sydney and there was genuine fear as most people assumed we were safe from such attacks.

Map of Terrorist Attacks

On the right we have a choropleth map of countries globally, colour coded by the number of terrorist attacks that country has experienced. It's nice to notice that Australia is on the lower end of the scale however alarming just North, Indonesia is on the higher end. Geographically, Australia is surrounded by other islands like itself, and an assumption can be made from this map that islands are less likely to experience terrorist attacks than inland countries.

I don't think any assumption can be made about which regions experience more attacks as it seems to be relatively dangerous countries in every region (perhaps Asia is more prone).

Relationship between #Killed and #Wounded

This bubble chart displays the relationship between number of people killed against number of people wounded from terrorist attacks. There is an apparent strong positive linear relationship.

Singapore: has experience very low people killed and wounded, a tribute to their national defense.

Japan and United States both have similar populations, however Japan and significantly better at defending against such attacks, even though US is renowned for their military force funding

Iraq: a country that has come out the worst in the world due to terrorism, with it's small population and such high death + wounded rate must be concerning and addressed.

Australia and Surrounding Countries

On the visualization on the right, we can compare Australia between other countries in a close proximity to Australia. This allows us to compare Australia to other countries in the Oceania continent and analyze how safe it is, relatively

As suspected, Australia does have a higher number of wounded people then number of people killed, as a result of terrorist attacks. Word Cloud of Terrorist Reports in 1970s USA

I thought with the social justice revolution taking place globally, having originated from USA, it would be interesting to see the language used in reports written about the terrorist attacks in USA during the 1970s.

It's interesting to note that words such as "Black" and "African" are used in relation to terrorism so often. This is reflective of the racial discrimination that is still apparent in USA against African Americans.

Some interesting words that intrigued me was "school" and "panther". School shootings are still terrorist attacks that occur frequently in USA today. I researched why panther was mentioned and to my surprise it was in relation to the now famous superhero Black Panther. The Black Panther Party was a famous racial justice group that was often accused of terrorist attack (which was false). It's interesting to note that the US government has still recorded these acts of terrorism.
